# So Will Be Your Company

## The Word at Seventeen

When I was 17 years old, I was in my room, praying, reading the Bible, and asking God, "What is it that He wants me to do?"

I was reading Mark 4:26-29:

> And He said, "So is the kingdom of God, as if a man should cast seed into the ground, and should sleep and rise night and day, and the seed should spring and grow; he knoweth not how.
>
> For the earth bringeth forth fruit of herself first the blade, then the ear, and then the full corn in the ear.
>
> But when the fruit is brought forth, immediately he putteth in the sickle, because the harvest is come."

Then He said in Mark 4:30-32:

> Whereunto shall we liken the kingdom of God, or with what comparison shall we compare it?
>
> It is like a grain of mustard seed, which, when it is sown in the earth, is less than all the seeds that be in the earth, but when it is sown, it groweth up and becometh greater than all the herbs, and shooteth out great branches so that the fowls of the air may nest under its shadow.

Then I heard the Lord tell me:

> So will be your company.

From that moment, I knew that I was called to be a businessman and to have a company.

Now I am 42 years old. For 25 years, I have wondered what it means that my business would be like a seed, like a grain of mustard seed that I would sow and that would grow, in a sense, on its own. I would not know how it was growing, and yet it would become greater than the other seeds and companies. It would become a fortress for other people, a place of protection.

## Discovering AI

About five years ago, I started working with AI. This was before ChatGPT, when AI was mostly tab completion through tools like GitHub Copilot or Tab9.

Ever since then, I have been a heavy user of AI. I started early with the major tools that came out, including ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, and others. I was always an early adopter.

Just recently, maybe only a couple of months ago, I began to realize that all AI is, in the end, really just sand taken from the ground. The processors responsible for inference are made out of silicon, and the other materials we need for memory and computation also come from the ground.

When people later started experimenting with loops like `/loop` in Claude Code or `/goal` in Codex, I experimented with them too. For the first time in my life using AI, I could see that these tools were capable of running for multiple hours, and sometimes even days. The longest task I have had running so far was, I think, 19 hours.

## The Prompt as Seed

Fast forward to Sunday, June 21, 2026. I was in church, walking in the hallway, and I suddenly realized something about the prophecy God gave me: my company would be like a mustard seed.

If you think of an AI model, such as GPT 5.5 or Opus 4.8, as the ground, then the prompt, the task that we give to these loops, is the seed.

When that process starts, it can be described in a way similar to Mark 4:27-28. You cast a seed on the ground; you give a large language model a task. Then it works on it autonomously for multiple hours, or even days.

You, as the developer, or as the farmer in the case of the scripture, do not know exactly how it is growing. Day and night it grows, and you do not know how.

## The Plan as DNA

Then, on the morning of June 23, 2026, I was walking outside, as I do every morning, praying and seeking God.

I suddenly realized that in the natural world, all the information about what the seed should become is already in the seed. The genetic information in the DNA provides the instructions for what the seed is supposed to become. It has direction, and it has the plan of what should be achieved.

The earth on its own does not have the plan, but it has the capability.

In the case of large language models, the model has access to the world's knowledge and knows how to build. But the creative part, and the place where the majority of human striving should be focused, is in building the seed: building the plan.

## Building the Seed

What I would like to build in this project is a system where the user can start by working on the plan.

The user can cast their vision and write down, or dictate, what they want to achieve. They can then iterate back and forth with AI to refine that vision, add more clarity, develop use cases, write user stories, create specifications, and do the thorough work of building the plan.

The user could then send this plan to a large language model through different harnesses, such as Codex, Claude Code, Pi coding agent, Cursor, and others.

The AI would then build the plan, but not as a black box where the user sees only a little bit of output. Instead, there would be a clear visual representation of:

- What the plan is.
- What the plan was.
- What the seed was.
- Which steps were specified in the seed.
- How those steps are progressing over time.

Users would be able to see, hour by hour, minute by minute, and day by day, how the plan is coming to life. A user could come back after an hour or two and see that the AI has progressed through different milestones.

## The Farmer

In the scripture from Mark 4, there is a seed and there is ground. But there are other important elements that the passage does not explicitly focus on. One of them is the farmer.

The farmer is not the one causing the seed to grow. The farmer is the one watching the whole process. Every now and then, he may make an executive decision. He may cut off leaves that do not belong to the plant, or he may add water. But overall, he is not the one making the seed grow.

In our system, the user also needs to be able to make executive decisions as the plan is progressing.

The ground, in our case the model or the engine, should also be able to ask the user questions or surface areas where it needs input from the user, the farmer.
